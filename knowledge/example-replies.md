### Follow-up: Client found emails in spam, asks how to prevent
**Client:** Found the emails in spam, asks how to avoid this happening.
**Reply:** If you click "Not Spam" on Google, it should learn that.

This is the full response from Google:

To Mark an Email as Not Spam (Immediate Fix)

Go to your Spam folder in Gmail.
Select the legitimate email(s).
Click the Not spam button at the top of the page.
The email will move to your inbox, and Gmail's AI learns to trust that sender.

To Prevent Future Spam Misclassifications

Add to Contacts: Open the email, click the sender's name/address, and select "Add to contacts" to create a safe sender list.
Create a Filter:
In Gmail Settings (gear icon) > See all settings > Filters and Blocked Addresses.
Click Create a new filter.
Enter the sender's email in the "From" field.
Click Create filter, then check "Never send to Spam" and Create filter again.
